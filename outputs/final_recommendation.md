## 1. Key Sales Drivers
*   **Local Advertising (Marketing Spend):** Highly significant positive association (+$1.21 sales per $1 spent). Shifting budget from discounting to local marketing is highly recommended.
*   **Staffing Levels:** Highly significant positive association (+$3,509.19 sales per additional staff member). Ensure optimal staffing to handle peak footfall.
*   **Competitor Clustering:** Proximity to competitors benefits sales (-$3,438.29 sales per km distance increase). Cluster store locations close to retail hubs.

---

## 2. Ineffective Variables
*   **Discounting Strategy:** The coefficient for average discount pct (**-41,271.77**) is statistically weak ($p = 0.224$). Discounting does not drive significant sales volume lift and should be scaled back.

---

## 3. Association vs. Causation Warning
Regression models capture statistical associations, not direct causation. For instance, staff headcount is associated with higher sales, but this may be because busy stores require more staff. True causation must be validated via localized experiments (A/B testing) before deploying massive labor increases.
